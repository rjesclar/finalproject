Caeleb Dressel

Olympic Games | World Championships | University of Florida

Caeleb Remel Dressel is a professional American swimmier who specializes in the sprint freestyle, butterfly, and medley events.
Dressel swims for the Cali Condors as part of the International Swimming League (ISL). He won seven gold medals while swimming
at the 2017 World Aquatic Championships, eight medals atthe 2018 World Championships, and 6 gold medals at the 2019 World
Championships. Dressel is a seven time olympic gold medalist and holds the world record in the 100 meter butterfly.

See Caeleb's Wikipedia Page.




  1. Olympic Games (2016, 2021)
    • Gold medalist as a member of the 4x100 freestyle relay and 4x100 medley relay (2016)
    • 6th place finisher in the 100 meter freestyle (2016)
    • Gold Medalist in the 50 meter freestyle (2021)
    • Gold Medalist in the 100 meter freestyle (2021)
    • Set a new world record and claimed olympic gold in the 100 meter butterfly (2021)
    • Set a new world record and claimed olympic gold as a member of the 4x100 medley relay (2021)
    • Achieved olympic gold as a member of the 4x100 meter free relay
    • Finished 5th as a member of 4x100 mixed medley relay

    • World champion as a member of the 4x100 meter medeley relay (2017)

    • World champion as a member of the 4x100 meter freestyle relay (2019)
